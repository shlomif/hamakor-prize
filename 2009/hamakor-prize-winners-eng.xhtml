Hamakor is the Israeli Society for Free and Open Source Software. Among the activities of the society, it gives the "Hamakor Prize" for contribution to open source in Israel, whose winners are determined by a vote by its friends and members. This year, the prize was given for several categories.

Better late than never, we wish to announce the winners in the Hamakor Prize for 2009, in the various categories:

  • The winner in the contribution to open-source category is Gábor Szabó (szabgab). Gabor has jump-started the community of Perl users in Israel; he set up an active mailing list, organised monthly meetings, as well as several Israeli conferences. In addition, he donated a lot of code and documentation for projects such as perl5, Parrot, Perl 6. He has several modules on CPAN and lately has initiated the Padre project for implementing an integrated development environment (IDE) for Perl and other languages, in Perl and is very active in its constant development and maintenance.

    Gábor manages Perl Technology Israel (PTI) which offers training courses in Perl, in Quality Assurance (QA) and in other fields in Israel and Internationally.

  • The winner in the contribution to the Israeli community is Shachar Shemesh.

    Shachar is one of the founding membes of "Hamakor" and served as a board member three times, including in the initiating board.

    Shachar is the owner and manager of Lingnu Open Source consulting Ltd., which provides services in the field of open-source software. Lingnu administers several of the servers of Hamakor.

    Shachar has also presented talks in the Haifa Linux Club, the Tel Aviv Open Source Club, the Herzelia Linux Developers Club, and the Jerusalem Linux club.

    Furthermore, Shachar has founded and is managing several free software projects such as rsyncrypto and fakeroot-ng and also has contributed to other open-source projects including WINE, a compatibility layer for Microsoft Windows.

  • The winner of the Shmu'el Yaniv Prize for Open Source and Free Software in the Localisation Category is Tomer Cohen. Tomer is concentrates the translation of Mozilla products in Israel, and is also active in advancing and publicising the Mozilla products in Israel and compatibility of sites to them. He manages a blog on his site that discusses Firefox, standards compatibility and other web-related topics.

    Part of Tomer's activity is as part of Mozilla Israel - mozilla.org.il, which is the Israeli community of the International Mozilla project, which is developing a large number of popular open-source applications.
